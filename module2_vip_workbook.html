Module 2: Oral Interaction and Service Simulation

Building confident restaurant communication through guided dialogues, role plays, and practical service scenarios.

Topic 2.0 Oral Interaction and Comprehensive Simulation of Food Service - 15h

Mode: Virtual synchronous with asynchronous support

You will be able to... welcome guests, take food and drink orders, recommend dishes confidently, solve simple service problems politely, and manage short group-service situations in English.

  • Subtopic 2.1: Customer reception and order taking.
  • Subtopic 2.2: Recommendations, suggestive selling, and daily specials.
  • Subtopic 2.3: Handling modifications, mistakes, and special requests.
  • Subtopic 2.4: Service for groups and complex situations.
  • Subtopic 2.5: Comprehensive simulation and oral assessment.

How this module works

The activities follow the same dynamic as Module 1, but now the visual style is warmer and more premium. Each dialogue includes listening, reading, writing, speaking, and reserved audio areas.

What students practice

  • Reception and taking orders
  • Food recommendations and suggestions
  • Solving mistakes and complaints
  • Group service interaction
  • Vocabulary reinforcement and final speaking performance

Dialogue 1

Reception and Taking Orders

Students practice greetings, drinks, and basic restaurant orders.
Listening
  1. Listen to the teacher reading the dialogue two times.
  2. Identify and write 3 greetings you hear.
  3. Listen again and write 2 drinks from the dialogue.
Audio space for Listening

Reserved area for the teacher audio or dialogue recording.

Reading
  1. Read the dialogue carefully.
  2. Underline all food and drink words.
  3. Circle all questions.
Dialogue

Waiter: Good evening. Welcome to our restaurant.

Customer: Good evening.

Waiter: Table for two?

Customer: Yes, please.

Waiter: This way, please. Here is your table.

Customer: Thank you.

Waiter: Here is the menu. Would you like something to drink?

Customer: Yes, I would like a lemonade, please.

Waiter: Of course. One lemonade. And for you?

Customer 2: I would like water, please.

Waiter: Still or sparkling water?

Customer 2: Still water, please.

Waiter: Perfect. I will come back to take your order.

(Later)

Waiter: Are you ready to order?

Customer: Yes. I would like chicken with rice.

Customer 2: I would like pasta.

Waiter: Excellent choice. Thank you.

Writing
  1. Write 3 sentences using: I would like...
  2. Write 1 short order with food and drink.
Audio space for Writing

Use this area for model answers, dictation, or pronunciation support.

Speaking - Role Play
  1. Work in pairs.
  2. Student A = Waiter / Student B = Customer.
  3. Practice the dialogue.
  4. Change roles.

Dialogue 2

Recommendations and Suggestions

Students recommend dishes and react to suggestions using simple adjectives.
Listening
  1. Listen to the dialogue.
  2. Write the recommended dish.
  3. Write 2 adjectives you hear.
Audio space for Listening

Reserved area for the recommendation dialogue audio.

Reading
  1. Read the dialogue.
  2. Underline all adjectives: delicious, fresh, popular.
  3. Answer: What is the special of the day?
Dialogue

Waiter: Are you ready to order?

Customer: Not yet. What do you recommend?

Waiter: I recommend the grilled fish. It is very fresh and delicious.

Customer: That sounds good. What comes with it?

Waiter: It comes with salad and rice.

Customer: Perfect. I will take that.

Waiter: Would you like something to drink?

Customer: Yes, a juice please.

Waiter: We also have today's special: beef soup.

Customer: Oh, that sounds interesting.

Waiter: It is very popular.

Customer: Ok, I will try the soup too.

Waiter: Great choice!

Writing
  1. Write 2 recommendations using I recommend...
  2. Write 1 sentence with Today's special is...

Dialogue 3

Problems and Solutions

Students respond politely to mistakes and offer simple solutions.
Listening
  1. Listen to the dialogue.
  2. Identify the problem.
  3. Write what the waiter says to solve it.
Audio space for Listening

Reserved area for a problem-solving dialogue recording.

Reading
  1. Read the dialogue.
  2. Underline all apology expressions.
Dialogue

Customer: Excuse me.

Waiter: Yes, how can I help you?

Customer: This is not my order. I ordered chicken, not fish.

Waiter: I'm very sorry for the mistake.

Customer: That's okay.

Waiter: I will fix it right away. Please wait a moment.

(Later)

Waiter: Here is your chicken. Sorry for the delay.

Customer: Thank you very much.

Waiter: Is everything okay now?

Customer: Yes, everything is fine.

Waiter: Enjoy your meal.

Writing
  1. Write 3 sentences with I'm sorry...
  2. Write 2 solutions. Example: I will fix it.

Dialogue 4

Group Service

Students simulate restaurant service with several customers and different orders.
Listening
  1. Listen and count how many people are in the group.
  2. Write 3 different orders.
Audio space for Listening

Reserved area for the group-service dialogue recording.

Reading
  1. Read the dialogue.
  2. Underline different foods and drinks.
Dialogue

Waiter: Hello everyone. Welcome!

Group: Hello.

Waiter: Are you ready to order?

Customer 1: Yes, I would like a burger and fries.

Waiter: And to drink?

Customer 1: A soda, please.

Waiter: Thank you. And you?

Customer 2: I would like a salad and water.

Waiter: Still or sparkling?

Customer 2: Still water.

Customer 3: I want pizza and a juice.

Waiter: Thank you. Anything else?

Group: No, that's all.

Waiter: Perfect. Your order will be ready soon.

Writing
  1. Write a group order for 4 people.
