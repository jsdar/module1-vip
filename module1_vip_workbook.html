Lesson 1: Greetings, Introductions and Professional Courtesy

Please, come with me to your table.

Here is the menu.

Thank you for coming.

The waiter is polite and friendly. The customer feels comfortable and happy.

Lesson 2

Lesson 2: Restaurant Protocol

Students identify the basic sequence of service in a restaurant.
Reading

The waiter greets the customer.

The waiter gives the menu.

The customer reads the menu.

The waiter takes the order.

The waiter brings food and drinks.

At the end, the waiter gives the bill.

Good service is important in a restaurant.

Focus Words
Menu Order Bill Table Service
Writing Game

  3. The waiter takes the ______.
  4. The customer pays the ______.

Lesson 3: Intercultural Communication

Students recognize the role of simple English in multicultural service.

English helps communication.

The customer understands and feels comfortable.

Water, please? Do you need help? Different languages Communication

Lesson 4

Lesson 4: Complaints and Special Diets

Students respond politely to problems and understand basic special-diet requests.
Reading

The customer has a problem.

The food is cold.

The waiter says: “I'm sorry.”

The waiter helps the customer.

Some customers have allergies.

They say: “No sugar” or “No gluten.”

The waiter must understand and help.

Focus Words
Sorry Cold No sugar No gluten Allergy
Writing Game
  1. I'm ______.
  2. The food is ______.
  3. No ______ please.

Word bank: sorry, cold, sugar

Lesson 5

Lesson 5: Menu and Food Description

Students describe simple dishes using basic adjectives.
Reading

This is the menu.

We have chicken, rice, and soup.

The food is hot and delicious.

The waiter describes the food:

“This is chicken with rice.”

“It is hot and very good.”

The customer chooses the food.

Food Words
Chicken Rice Soup Hot Delicious
Writing Game

  2. It is ______.
  3. The food is ______.
